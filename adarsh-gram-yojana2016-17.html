Student Volunteer for the 'Adarsh Gram Yojana Project' (2016-17)

FICCI FLO Chennai Chapter 2016-17, under the Gram Yojana Scheme, has adopted four villages namely Venkatapuram, Sasthirambakkam, Thellimedu and Mettu colony belonging to the Venkatapuram Panchayat, Kanchipuram District, under the Adarsh Gram Yojana Project. The project, titled 'Smart Village Project', is structured for 3 years, and based on a need-based survey conducted by us at the village.

The mission is to make this village a model, smart village by undertaking the following:
  1. 100% open defecation-free village
  2. Facilitation of after-school classes in Math and English.
  3. Offering medical services
  4. Achieving a carbon–free environment through the plantation of trees.

After need assessments, discussions with the Panchayat leaders, and talks with experts in the field, the Phase 1 activities were narrowed down to - education, after-school support to children, health and hygiene, and sanitation. Educated teachers are employed to teach the school children and to help them during exams. Yoga classes are being incorporated in their school timetable once a week to help students de-stress and relax. This has proven to help the mind-body connect of some students.

Based on a survey, it is understood that around 600 houses in the entire Panchayat do not have toilets. They use a common toilet, which is located very far away from their homes. Sometimes, difference in castes also stands in the way of all villagers using the toilet – persons of a particular caste are allowed to use the toilet before others.

Personally, I have been involved in teaching math to students in two of the four villages. I am delighted to say that the school reports have shown considerable improvement in math over the course of 6 months due to the efforts of all the after-school teachers and volunteers.
